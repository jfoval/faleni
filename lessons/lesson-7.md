# Faleni ¬∑ Lesson 7 ‚Äî Feelings & health

Past the core course, and the payoff of Faleni's design shows: this whole lesson
adds **no new grammar**. Every feeling word belongs to the **`m-` family** (mind &
feeling), so once you know the prefix you can half-guess the word ‚Äî and every
health word is either one you've met or a compound you can already build
(`pa kose` doctor = *person + fix*). You'll say how you feel, ask why, and get
through a visit to the doctor.

## How do you feel? ‚Äî the `m-` family

Every word for a feeling starts with **`m`** (the mind & feeling family), so a new
one is rarely a surprise. To ask, use `jefe` (how):

- `we ha mati jefe?` ‚Äî How do you feel? (*you do feel how?*)
- `wa ha masu` ‚Äî I'm happy. ¬∑ `wa ha moli` ‚Äî I'm sad.

Here's the feeling family at a glance ‚Äî all guessable once you hear the `m-`:

| Faleni | Feeling | Faleni | Feeling |
|---|---|---|---|
| `masu` | happy | `moli` | sad |
| `mafa` | angry | `maji` | calm |
| `mupi` | afraid | `mana` | worried |
| `mame` | lonely | `mahi` | grateful |
| `mafi` | proud | `maha` | surprised |

Negate any of them with `he` (Lesson 2):

- `wa ha he moli` ‚Äî I'm not sad. ¬∑ `wa ha maji` ‚Äî I'm calm.

**Asking *why*** uses `jesa` (why); answer with `uka` (because):

- `we ha moli jesa?` ‚Äî Why are you sad?
- `uka wa ha mame` ‚Äî Because I'm lonely.

## Love, like & hate

- `mu` covers *love* and *like*: `wa ha mu we` ‚Äî I love you. ¬∑
  `wa ha mu sope` ‚Äî I like dogs.
- Its opposite is `maja` (hate): `wa ha maja lika` ‚Äî I hate the cold.
- A ready-made compound from your cheat-sheet: `kipa mu` ‚Äî *start + love* =
  **fall in love**.

## More or less ‚Äî feelings can compare

Feelings take the same comparison you learned for size (Lesson 4 / Numbers):
`nelo` (more), `nela` (most), `ame` (than).

- `wa ha masu nelo ame neke` ‚Äî I'm happier than yesterday.
- `wa ha maji nela` ‚Äî I'm the calmest.

> Faleni has no separate word for *very* ‚Äî reach for `nelo` (more) or compare.

## Not feeling well

Three states you'll want first ‚Äî `lunu` (tired) you already know from Lesson 2:

- `wa ha lefa` ‚Äî I'm sick. ¬∑ `wa ha lunu` ‚Äî I'm tired. ¬∑ `wa ha lulu` ‚Äî I'm well.

**Where it hurts** uses the body (`p-` family). There's no special word for *pain*
‚Äî you say a part **feels bad**, `mati lo` (*feel + bad*):

- `pika` head ¬∑ `pele` belly/stomach ¬∑ `pipu` throat ¬∑ `piko` heart ¬∑ `pete` ear ¬∑
  `pefa` tooth
- `pika wa ha mati lo` ‚Äî My head hurts. (*my head feels bad*)
- `pele wa ha mati lo` ‚Äî My stomach hurts.

## At the doctor

Every "medical" word is a **`kose` (fix)** compound you already know how to read:

- `pa kose` ‚Äî doctor (*person + fix*) ¬∑ `te kose` ‚Äî medicine (*thing + fix*) ¬∑
  `tepe kose` ‚Äî hospital (*building + fix*)
- `wa ha meni pa kose` ‚Äî I need a doctor. (`meni` = need)
- `wa ha ka nosa tepe kose` ‚Äî I go to the hospital.
- `we ha melo kepi wa?` ‚Äî Can you help me? (`melo` = can, `kepi` = help)

**Getting better** uses `ke` (make) and `nesi` (soon):

- `te kose ha ke lulu we` ‚Äî Medicine makes you well.
- `we ha lulu nesi` ‚Äî You'll be well soon.

## A little conversation ‚Äî at the doctor

> **A:** `fofu, wa ha lefa.` ‚Äî Excuse me, I'm sick.
> **B:** `we ha mati jefe?` ‚Äî How do you feel?
> **A:** `pika wa ha mati lo, hi wa ha lunu.` ‚Äî My head hurts, and I'm tired.
> **B:** `we ha meni kumi hi te kose.` ‚Äî You need sleep and medicine.
> **A:** `wa ha lulu nesi ho?` ‚Äî Will I be well soon?
> **B:** `awe. we ha lulu nesi.` ‚Äî Yes. You'll be well soon.
> **A:** `fife! la ka!` ‚Äî Thanks! Goodbye!

### 2. Read & understand

Read each aloud, then reveal the meaning.

- `wa ha mafa uka wa ha lunu.`
- `pele we ha mati lo ho?`
- `we ha melo kepi wa? wa ha lefa.`

<details><summary>Meanings</summary>

1. I'm angry because I'm tired.
2. Does your stomach hurt?
3. Can you help me? I'm sick.
</details>

### 3. Say it in Faleni

Translate to Faleni:
1. I'm happy today.
2. Why are you worried?
3. My throat hurts.
4. I love my family.
5. You'll be well soon.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

2. `we ha mana jesa?`
3. `pipu wa ha mati lo`
4. `wa ha mu pamu wa`
