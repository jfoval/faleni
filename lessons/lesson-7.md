# Faleni · Lesson 7 — Feelings & health

Past the core course, and the payoff of Faleni's design shows: this whole lesson
adds **no new grammar**. Every feeling word belongs to the **`m-` family** (mind &
feeling), so once you know the prefix you can half-guess the word — and every
health word is either one you've met or a compound you can already build
(`pa kose` doctor = *person + fix*). You'll say how you feel, ask why, and get
through a visit to the doctor.

---

## How do you feel? — the `m-` family

Every word for a feeling starts with **`m`** (the mind & feeling family), so a new
one is rarely a surprise. To ask, use `jefe` (how):

- `we ha mati jefe?` — How do you feel? (*you do feel how?*)
- `wa ha masu` — I'm happy. · `wa ha moli` — I'm sad.

Here's the feeling family at a glance — all guessable once you hear the `m-`:

| Faleni | Feeling | Faleni | Feeling |
|---|---|---|---|
| `masu` | happy | `moli` | sad |
| `mafa` | angry | `maji` | calm |
| `mupi` | afraid | `mana` | worried |
| `mame` | lonely | `mahi` | grateful |
| `mafi` | proud | `maha` | surprised |

Negate any of them with `he` (Lesson 2):

- `wa ha he moli` — I'm not sad. · `wa ha maji` — I'm calm.

**Asking *why*** uses `jesa` (why); answer with `uka` (because):

- `we ha moli jesa?` — Why are you sad?
- `uka wa ha mame` — Because I'm lonely.

## Love, like & hate

- `mu` covers *love* and *like*: `wa ha mu we` — I love you. ·
  `wa ha mu sope` — I like dogs.
- Its opposite is `maja` (hate): `wa ha maja lika` — I hate the cold.
- A ready-made compound from your cheat-sheet: `kipa mu` — *start + love* =
  **fall in love**.

## More or less — feelings can compare

Feelings take the same comparison you learned for size (Lesson 4 / Numbers):
`nelo` (more), `nela` (most), `ame` (than).

- `wa ha masu nelo ame neke` — I'm happier than yesterday.
- `wa ha maji nela` — I'm the calmest.

> Faleni has no separate word for *very* — reach for `nelo` (more) or compare.

## Not feeling well

Three states you'll want first — `lunu` (tired) you already know from Lesson 2:

- `wa ha lefa` — I'm sick. · `wa ha lunu` — I'm tired. · `wa ha lulu` — I'm well.

**Where it hurts** uses the body (`p-` family). There's no special word for *pain*
— you say a part **feels bad**, `mati lo` (*feel + bad*):

- `pika` head · `pele` belly/stomach · `pipu` throat · `piko` heart · `pete` ear ·
  `pefa` tooth
- `pika wa ha mati lo` — My head hurts. (*my head feels bad*)
- `pele wa ha mati lo` — My stomach hurts.

## At the doctor

Every "medical" word is a **`kose` (fix)** compound you already know how to read:

- `pa kose` — doctor (*person + fix*) · `te kose` — medicine (*thing + fix*) ·
  `tepe kose` — hospital (*building + fix*)
- `wa ha meni pa kose` — I need a doctor. (`meni` = need)
- `wa ha ka nosa tepe kose` — I go to the hospital.
- `we ha melo kepi wa?` — Can you help me? (`melo` = can, `kepi` = help)

**Getting better** uses `ke` (make) and `nesi` (soon):

- `te kose ha ke lulu we` — Medicine makes you well.
- `we ha lulu nesi` — You'll be well soon.

## A little conversation — at the doctor

> **A:** `fofu, wa ha lefa.` — Excuse me, I'm sick.
> **B:** `we ha mati jefe?` — How do you feel?
> **A:** `pika wa ha mati lo, hi wa ha lunu.` — My head hurts, and I'm tired.
> **B:** `we ha meni kumi hi te kose.` — You need sleep and medicine.
> **A:** `wa ha lulu nesi ho?` — Will I be well soon?
> **B:** `awe. we ha lulu nesi.` — Yes. You'll be well soon.
> **A:** `fife! la ka!` — Thanks! Goodbye!

## Practice

### 1. Listen & choose

Tap 🔊, listen, then pick what it means.

::quiz
we ha mati jefe = How do you feel? | Why are you sad? | Can you help me?
wa ha he moli = I'm not sad | I'm not tired | I'm not angry
pika wa ha mati lo = My head hurts | My heart is calm | My tooth is fine
wa ha meni pa kose = I need a doctor | I need medicine | I need sleep
::

### 2. Read & understand

Read each aloud, then reveal the meaning.

- `wa ha mafa uka wa ha lunu.`
- `pele we ha mati lo ho?`
- `we ha melo kepi wa? wa ha lefa.`

<details><summary>Meanings</summary>

1. I'm angry because I'm tired.
2. Does your stomach hurt?
3. Can you help me? I'm sick.
</details>

### 3. Say it in Faleni

Translate to Faleni:
1. I'm happy today.
2. Why are you worried?
3. My throat hurts.
4. I love my family.
5. You'll be well soon.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

1. `wa ha masu neto`
2. `we ha mana jesa?`
3. `pipu wa ha mati lo`
4. `wa ha mu pamu wa`
5. `we ha lulu nesi`
</details>
