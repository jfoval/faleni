# Faleni · Lesson 8 — Work, study & getting around town

Deeper into "going further," and again the design does the heavy lifting: this
whole lesson adds **no new grammar**. Jobs are **`pa` (person)** compounds you can
already read (`pa fapi` teacher = *person + teach*), study words cluster in the
**`f-` family** (speech & info), and getting around town runs on the **`k-` family**
(actions & motion) plus a few place words in **`t-`**. You'll talk about your job,
your studies, and how to cross town.

## Talking about your job — the `pa` family

An occupation is almost always **`pa` (person) + what they do**, head-first, so you
can build most jobs the moment you know the verb:

- `wa ha towa` — I work. (`towa` = work, Lesson 6) · `wa ha pa towa` — I'm a worker.
- `pa fapi wa` — my teacher (*person + teach*, then owner `wa`; bare owner-after-noun, Lesson 3)

Here's the job family at a glance — every one is guessable from the verb inside it:

| Faleni | Job | Faleni | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| `pa fapi` | teacher | `pa famu` | student |
| `pa kose` | doctor | `pa kupe` | cook |
| `pa fano` | writer | `pa femi` | scientist |
| `pa kuku` | farmer | `pa kifu` | boss / leader |

Your workplace is a **place word** (`ti` place, Lesson 5): `ti towa` — office
(*place + work*), `tepe famu` — school building (*building + learn*). Ask what
someone does with `jeta` (what, Lesson 3) — the question word sits in the answer
slot, right where the reply will go:

- `we ha ke towa jeta?` — What work do you do? (*you do work what?*)
- `wa ha pa kupe. wa ha towa ti tepo` — I'm a cook. I work at the shop.

## Money, hours & the workday

Work talk reuses money and time words you already have — no new roots:

- `wa ha kani tomi` — I earn money. (`kani` = get, so `kani tomi` = earn money)
- `pa kifu ha ko tomi nosa wa` — the boss pays me. (*gives money to me*)
- `wa ha kipa towa nami` — I start work in the morning. (`kipa` = start, Lesson 6)
- `wa ha kine towa nana` — I finish work in the evening. (`kine` = end/finish)

Two everyday states, both in the `l-` family (qualities): `lose` (busy) and
`lunu` (tired, Lesson 2):

- `wa ha lose neto` — I'm busy today. · `wa ha lunu nusu towa` — I'm tired from work.

## At school — the `f-` family

Study words gather under **`f`** (speech & info), so a new one rarely surprises you.
The two anchors, `famu` (learn) and `fapi` (teach), you already know:

- `wa ha famu faleni ti famu` — I study Faleni at school.
- `pa fapi ha fapi wa` — the teacher teaches me.
- `wa ha fi fila` — I read a book. (`fi` = read, `fila` = book)
- `wa ha fano fe lewi` — I write new words. (`fano` = write, `fe` = word, Lesson 3)

A quick pair to keep straight: `ti famu` is **the school** (the institution),
`tepe famu` is **the school building** (*building + learn*) — same `famu` inside,
different head.

| Faleni | At school | Faleni | At school |
|---|---|---|---|
| `fewa` | ask | `fewo` | answer |
| `feji` | explain | `mepe` | understand |
| `fipe` | translate | `fele` | math |
| `femi` | science | `feli` | history |

When you're stuck, two `m-` words (mind family, Lesson 7) carry you:

- `wa ha mepe` — I understand. · `wa ha he mepe` — I don't understand. (`he`, Lesson 2)
- `we ha melo feji asa?` — Can you explain again? (`melo` = can, Lesson 7)

> No single word for *homework* — build it: `towa famu` = *work + learn* =
> study-work, a task you take home.

## Getting around — the `k-` family

Every motion word starts with **`k`** (the actions & motion family). The first four
come as pairs:

- `ka` (go) · `kawi` (come) — you know these from Lesson 3.
- `kihi` (arrive) · `kihu` (leave) — reach a place or set off from one.
- `kali` (walk) · `kawu` (run) · `kiki` (drive) · `kejo` (ride) — how you travel.

Point where you're going with `nosa` (to) and where you came from with `nusu`
(from), both from Lesson 5:

- `wa ha ka nosa tuna` — I go to the city. · `wa ha kawi nusu tu` — I come from home.
- `wa ha kali tewi nosa towa` — I commute to work. (`kali tewi` = *walk + road* = travel)

A few place words for crossing town — all ones you can read at a glance:

- `tu tomi` — bank (*house + money*) · `ti taji loni` — station (*place + train*)
- `tepo` — shop · `ti towa` — office · `tuna` — the city itself

Transport modes are built head-first from `taji` (vehicle):

- `taji` — car · `taji loni` — train (*car + long*) · `taji le` — bus (*car + big*)
- `teku kuwi` — airplane (*tool + fly*) · `taje` — boat

## Asking the way

Ask where something is with `jewa` (where, Lesson 5) and how to get there with
`jefe` (how) — both question words sit in the answer slot, and the sentence keeps
its full **subject + `ha` + predicate** frame:

- `ti famu ha ti jewa?` — Where is the school? (*the school is at where?*)
- `wa ha ka nosa tepo jefe?` — How do I get to the shop? (*I go to shop how?*)
- `niju ho?` — Is it near? (`niju` = near) · `nopa ho?` — Is it far? (`nopa` = far)

Directions run on `kefo` (turn) plus the left/right pair, and on `nihu` (ahead):

- `kefo nohu` — turn left (*turn + left*) · `kefo nono` — turn right
- `ka nihu` — go straight ahead (*go + front*) · `kuti ti wo` — stop here
- `felu ha kule tewi` — the map shows the road. (`kule` = show, `felu` = map)

---

## A little conversation — asking directions

> **A:** `fofu, ti taji loni ha ti jewa?` — Excuse me, where is the train station?
> **B:** `niju. ka nihu, hi kefo nono.` — It's near. Go straight ahead, and turn right.
> **A:** `wa ha melo kali nosa wu ho?` — Can I walk there?
> **B:** `awe. tewi ha loti. kali liwa.` — Yes. The road is short. Walk fast.
> **A:** `tefo kilu ha tomi jenu?` — How much is a ticket?
> **B:** `wa ha he ma. fewa pa towa ti wu.` — I don't know. Ask the worker there.
> **A:** `fife! la ka!` — Thanks! Goodbye!

### 2. Read & understand

Read each aloud, then reveal the meaning.

- `wa ha pa fapi. wa ha towa ti famu.`
- `wa ha ka nosa tu tomi jefe?`
- `wa ha he mepe. feji asa, foso.`

1. I'm a teacher. I work at the school.
2. How do I get to the bank?
3. I don't understand. Explain again, please.
</details>

### 3. Say it in Faleni

Translate to Faleni:
1. I study Faleni.
2. Where is the office?
3. I drive to work.
4. Turn left, and go straight ahead.
5. The teacher teaches new words.

<details><summary>Answers</summary>

2. `ti towa ha ti jewa?`
3. `wa ha kiki nosa towa`
4. `kefo nohu, hi ka nihu`
5. `pa fapi ha fapi fe lewi`
</details>
